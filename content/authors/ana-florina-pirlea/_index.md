Ana Florina Pirlea is a Statistician in the World Bank’s Development Data Group, where she works on the World Development Indicators and the creation of data-driven narratives. Her work focuses on data communication and visualization. She is also involved in database management, particularly indicators related to business environment, infrastructure, and governance. Florina holds a master’s degree in International Economics and International Law from Johns Hopkins University (SAIS) and a bachelor’s degree in Economics and Psychology from Bryn Mawr College.
